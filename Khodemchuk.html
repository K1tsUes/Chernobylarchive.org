Historical biography

Valery Khodemchuk

As the Senior Main Circulation Pump Operator of the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant, his presence at the absolute epicenter of the blast on the night of April 26, 1986, and the fact that his remains were forever entombed within the ruins made him the very first casualty and one of the most tragic, symbolic figures of the global nuclear disaster.

Birth: 24th of March 1953
Death: 26th of April 1986

First Years and Formation

Valery Ilich Khodemchuk was born on March 24, 1951, in the rural village of Kropyvnia, located in the Ivankiv region(Oblast of Kiev, actual Ukraine). Raised in a humble, post-war Soviet enviroment, he developed a discipined work ethic and deep interest in industrial mechanics early in his youth. Following his mandatory service in the Soviet Armed Forces, he pursued specialized vocational education in heavy-duty thermal energy pumping systems, which ultimately paved his way into the prestigious Soviet nuclear program.

Chernobyl Carreer

Khodemchuk joined the Chernobyl Power Plant in September 1973, making him one of the station's most senior and experienced operations workers who actively participated in the construction and comisioning of the first three reactor units. Due to his exceptional reliability and technical expertise, he was steadily promoted through the ranks, eventually becoming a Group 7 Senior Operator. When reactor 4 became operational in 1983, Khodemchuk was assigned to oversee its massive main circulation pumps, moving to the nearby city of Pripyat to live a quiet life with his wife, Natalya, and their two children, Oleg and Larissa.

Day of the disaster

On the night of April 25-26, 1986, Khodemchuk was clocked in for the regular midnight shift. Minutes before of the disaster, during the ill-fated safety test, he was ordered to go into the Nothern Main Cirucation Pump Hall to report on how machinery was vibrating under the test conditions. At 1:23:45 AM, Reactor 4 suffered two catastrophic steam and chemical explosions that instantly vaporized the pumping Hall, killing him in the act due to the structural collapse and massive thermal trauma. Despite desperate and heroic shearch attemps through blinding radioactive steam by his supervisor Valery Perevozchenko and the Senior Mechanical Engineer Aleksandr Yuvchenko, Khodemchuk's body could never be reached; his remains were forever entombed beneath the collapsed 2000-ton upper biological shield and tons of concrete, where he rests to this day under the sarcophagus. There is a memorial monument at the entrance of unit 4.
