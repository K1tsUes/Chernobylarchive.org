Historical biography

Anatoly Kurguz

As the Senior Reactor Operator in charge of the central hall at the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant, his role on the night of April 26, 1986, and his heroic actions following the explosions—including closing a massive blast door to shield his colleagues from lethal, radioactive steam—made him one of the most critical and tragic figures of the nuclear disaster.

Birth: 12th of June 1957
Death: 12th of May 1986

First Years and Formation

Anatoly Kharlampiovych Kurguz was born on June 12, 1957, in Krasnovichi, a village located in the Bryansk Oblast of western Russia. From a young age, he showed a strong interest in technical and mechanical sciences. In 1977, he graduated from the Pechora River School, specializing in Navigation on Inland Waterways and Operation of Naval Power Plants. Following his graduation, he fulfilled his mandatory military service in the Soviet Navy's Pacific Fleet before transitioning his expertise to the nuclear industry.

Chernobyl Carreer

In the early 1980s, he began his career at the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant. Due to his exceptional focus and reliable technical performance, he was promoted to the position of Senior Reactor Operator (OICB). In this role, he was responsible for supervising operations and monitoring vital pressure systems inside the central hall of Reactor 4.

Day of the disaster

During the night of April 26, 1986, Kurguz was stationed at his post in the central hall while the safety test was underway. When the reactor exploded at 01:23, the blast ruptured the main cooling circuits, immediately filling his workspace with radioactive debris and high-pressure steam. Despite being severely burned and blinded by the heat, he focused entirely on saving his fellow workers.

Mitigation actions and Death

After the explosions, Kurguz forced himself to close a massive blast door to block the lethal steam, which shielded his colleague Oleg Genrikh and other workers from a fatal exposure. He then crawled through the ruins to ensure others were evacuating before seeking medical help. Kurguz received a massive radiation dose of 6.7 Gy compounded by thermal burns, and died on May 12, 1986, at Hospital Number 6 in Moscow.
