Historical biography

Vladimir Shashenok

As an Automation Systems Adjuster for the Atomenergonaladka enterprise at the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant, his role on the night of April 26, 1986, and his tragic fate following the explosions—being crushed by collapsing structures and severely scalded by high-pressure radioactive steam while monitoring pressure gauges—made him one of the most tragic figures of the nuclear disaster.

Birth: 21st of April 1951
Death: 26th of April 1986

First Years and Formation

Vladimir Nikolaevich Shashenok was born on April 21, 1951, in the village of Shchuchyn, located within the Chernihiv Oblast of the Ukrainian SSR. Displaying a natural aptitude for mechanics and precise measurements during his youth, he sought a specialized technical path. He enrolled at the Konotop Industrial Technical School, where he successfully graduated and developed the expertise in industrial automation required for the Soviet Union's advancing energy infrastructures.

Chernobyl Carreer

Following his education, Shashenok joined the specialized startup and adjustment enterprise known as Atomenergonaladka, which deployed expert engineers to nuclear projects. This career path brought him directly to the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant, where he was trusted with adjusting, maintaining, and monitoring the delicate automatic instrumentation and measurement systems vital to the operational safety of the RBMK reactors.

Day of the disaster

During the night of April 26, 1986, Shashenok was stationed in Room 604 on Level +24, positioned directly under the reactor's feedwater system. At 01:23 AM, when the reactor suffered its catastrophic power surge and exploded, the massive overpressure shockwave shattered the room. Shashenok was immediately buried under a collapsing ceiling beam, while the ruptured pipes completely filled his confined workspace with blinding, high-pressure radioactive steam.

Mitigation actions and Death

Buried under debris and heavily scalded, Shashenok was found unconscious by a rescue team consisting of Pyotr Palamarchuk and Nikolai Gorbachenko, who carried him out of the flooded wreckage. He was rushed to Pripyat Sanitary Unit No. 126, suffering from severe thermal and radiation burns covering his entire body, as well as a fractured spine and broken ribs. Vladimir Shashenok passed away at 06:00 AM on April 26, 1986, becoming the second fatality of the disaster. He was posthumously awarded the Soviet Order of the Badge of Honour in 1986 and the Ukrainian Cross for Courage in 1996.
