Localities of the Chernobyl Exclusion Zone

Abandoned settlements following the 1986 disaster

[ UKRANIAN SECTOR ]

  • [ PRIPYAT ]

    A ghost town founded in 1970 to house the nuclear power plant workers. It had a population of nearly 50,000 residents before its total emergency evacuation.

    COORDINATES: 51º24'18.0933"N, 30º03'10.0504"E
    Access Street Map & POI »

  • [ Chernobyl ]

    An ancient historic town that gives its name to the power plant. Today, operates as the main administrative hub for technical zone supervision and hosts shift workers.

    COORDINATES: 51º16'35.5137"N, 30º13'15.0794"E
    Access Street Map & POI »

  • [ Kopachi ]

    A village located in close proximity to the reactor. Its wooden structures became so highly contaminated that the Soviet military completely demolished and buried them underground

    Kopachi — Buried village: only the kindergarten remains standing.(51°21'26.2"N 30°07'19.1"E)

  • [ Zalissya ]

    The largest village within the zone with over 3,000 residents. Following the disaster, it was entirely deserted and has since been reclaimed by nature

    COORDINATES: 50º37'49.3171"N, 30º52'21.2200"E
    Access Street Map & POI »

[ BIELORUSSIAN SECTOR ]

  • [ KHOYNIKI ]

    Located at the edge of the bielorussian exclusion zone, this town serves as the primary technical access point and monitoring station for the heavily contaminated southern Belarussian territories

    COORDINATES: 51º54'40.3581"N, 30º00'0.3573"E
    Access Street Map & POI »

    [ Naroulya ]

    A regional municipality heavily affected by radioactive fallout. Dozens of its collective agricultural communes were completely abandoned and integrated into the radiation reserve.

    COORDINATES: 51º48'3.0477"N, 29º29'49.6712"E
    Access Street Map & POI »

    [ Krasnoselye ]

    An evacuated settlement featuring a famous 35-meter Soviet fire observation tower and an abandoned water pumping station, now deep within the restricted scientific reserve territory

    COORDINATES: 51º33'33.8971"N, 29º54'1.1663E
    Access Street Map & POI »

  • [ Dronki ]

    A heavily contaminated agrarian village deep in the Polesia core zone. Its decaying structures stand today as an active field site for radioecological research and wildlife monitoring.

    COORDINATES: 51º38'47.53190"N 29º52'47.6383"E
    Access Street Map & POI »
