Historical biography

Leonid Toptunov

As the Senior Reactor Control Engineer (SIUR) of the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant, his role on the night of April 26, 1986, and his heroic actions following the explosions made him one of the most critical and tragic figures of the nuclear disaster.

Birth: 16th of August 1960
Death: 14th of May 1986

First Years and Formation

Leonid Fyodorovych Toptunov was born in Mykolayivka, a town located in the Sumy Oblast of northeastern Ukraine. From a young age, he showed a strong interest in physics and the exact sciences. In 1983, he graduated from the prestigious Moscow Power Engineering Institute (MPEI), specializing in nuclear power facilities engineering.

Chernobyl Carreer

In March 1983, he started his career at the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant. In just three years, he was promoted due to his outstanding technical capabilities to hold the position of Senior Reactor Control Engineer (SIUR). In this role, he operated the main reactor control board inside Main Control Room 4 (MCR-4), working directly with the Selsyn indicators and the MTK mnemonic channel displays.

Day of the disaster

During the night of April 26, 1986, Toptunov was operating the reactor while a safety test was being prepared. Following an unexpected drop in reactor power, and under the direct orders of his shift supervisor, Aleksandr Akimov (overseen by Anatoly Dyatlov), he attempted to stabilize the power by withdrawing control rods from the core. This action left the reactor in an extremely unstable configuration. Minutes later, Toptunov pressed the AZ-5 button to shut down the reactor, but a severe design flaw in the control rods triggered a massive power surge that caused the catastrophic explosions.

Mitigation actions and Death

After the explosions, Leonid remained at the facilities. He went with Akimov to room 714/2, where the valves whose function was to open the water flow to the reactor were located. They were later joined in this effort by Vyacheslav Orlov, Arkady Uskov, and Anatoly Nekhayev. Unfortunately, Leonid and Aleksandr received a lethal radiation dose estimated between 10 and 15 Gy. Toptunov died on May 14, 1986, due to Acute Radiation Syndrome at Hospital Number 6 in Moscow.
